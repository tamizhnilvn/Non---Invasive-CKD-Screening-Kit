# Non---Invasive-CKD-Screening-Kit
A Screening Kit for CKD (Cronic Kidney Disease) which is robust, compact, non - invasive and cost less than an average Screening process of CKD. Mainly helps for patients in rural areas and remote places where they can test within seconds.
Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a progressive disorder that gradually impairs renal function, often remaining undetected until advanced stages. Traditional screening methods using serum creatinine, glomerular filtration rate, and urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio are resource-intensive and unsuitable for large-scale use in rural healthcare settings. This work presents a non-invasive CKD screening kit that detects ammonia (NH₃) levels in exhaled breath, leveraging the clinical correlation between elevated breath ammonia and reduced kidney function. The system integrates an MQ135 ammonia sensor and a DHT22 temperature-humidity sensor with an ESP32 microcontroller. Environmental compensation algorithms correct ammonia readings for temperature and humidity variations, enhancing accuracy and reliability. Results are displayed on an OLED module or a web dashboard, providing quick and remote-access screening outputs. Ammonia levels are classified into normal (<5 ppm), mild risk (5–12 ppm), and high risk (>12 ppm) categories. The device provides rapid analysis within two minutes and is designed for an affordable production cost of ₹1,000–₹2,000. Preliminary evaluations and literature analysis indicate a diagnostic sensitivity of 69–80%, demonstrating the system's potential as a low-cost, point-of-care CKD risk screening tool.
